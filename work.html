Where is Brian?

It’s impolite to talk too much about oneself — so excuse the French arrogance. But to give you an idea of what I do: my work as a translator quickly became more than just words. I began acting as a bridge, helping clients connect with French savoir-faire and savoir-vivre.

---

Living near Paris led me into unique, sometimes unexpected projects. I was asked to help trace missing gold at the Banque de France, tied to the RMS Republic treasure, working directly with Captain Martin Bayerle.

🏴‍☠️ Soon after, I was deep in the archives of the Bibliothèque Richelieu, assisting a New York researcher searching for forgotten French texts. Then came the arts. A filmmaker needed a painter in Paris — I found the perfect one. 📽️
Around that time, I just wanted a Hugh Grant haircut from Love Actually. But life had other plans: I became a different kind of Hugh — the personal agent of a Playmate model during Paris Fashion Week. 📽️

In between, I’ve managed private homes, supported legal matters for foreign clients, and acted as a discreet liaison. One case involved assisting a man from Ottawa who believed he was facing language discrimination — I helped him defend his right to speak French.
Each assignment is different — translation, research, cultural scouting, personal support — but the goal is always the same: to bring you more of France.
